Aloe barbadensis miller

  • Home

Family:Asphodelaceae

Common Name:Aloe vera
Distribution :: Generally recognized that the origin is Arabia, Somalia or Sudan. A. vera is widely distributed throughout the tropics and subtropics, mostly known as a cultivated or naturalized plant
Flowering Period:spring, summer, fall
Habitat :Human altered environment (yards, fields), It is grown in outdoors in warm temperate zones and subtropical and tropical areas.
Key Characters
Plant type:A. vera is a perennial, long-lived, succulent plant. It takes 4-5 years to mature and can live up to 25 years (Akinyele and Odiyi, 2007).
Leaves :Lance-shaped, thick, pointed and freshy- green leaves
Flowers:Bisexual/unisexual; Inflorescence or not, no of sepal, petal, stamen and carpels (ovary type) details)
Uses :A. vera is widely cultivated because of its commercial value. It is used in medicinal, food, cosmetic and pharmaceutical uses (Akinyele and Odiyi, 2007; Fern, 2014; PROTA, 2017; USDA-ARS, 2017). Psoriasis, Seborrhoea, Dandruff , Minor burns, Skin abrasions, Skin injured by radiation, Herpes sores, Acne, Anal fissures.
